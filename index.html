Lifegard II Electronics

Motion-alarm behavior, rebuilt for modern prop bays.

A modern recreation inspired by the motion-alarm behavior of original 1980s and 1990s Lifegard II hardware. Built for prop makers, cosplayers, collectors, and display builders who want responsive lights, sound, and selectable operating modes inside their own custom body.

  • Safe, Armed, and Alarm/Test behavior with motion and stillness sensing, status LEDs, and buzzer feedback.
  • Modern prop electronics for cosplay, display, and hobby builds; this is not original Lifegard II hardware.
  • Not certified or intended for real fire, rescue, medical, emergency, monitoring, or safety use.

Electronics only: no shell included.Neither version includes a Lifegard II shell, case, enclosure, outer body, printed housing, mounting hardware, or cosmetic exterior pieces. You must provide, fabricate, print, modify, or source your own shell.

FAQ

Workshop notes.

Are these ready to ship?

Availability and lead time are shown on the individual Ecwid product listing. Some items may be ready-made, while small-batch builds may require assembly and testing time before shipment.

Can I request custom LED colors?

Custom LED color requests are limited to the top 7 LEDs on the Belt Gizmo and the 16 perimeter LEDs around the static LED on the Frozen Empire-inspired Version 1 Pocket Gizmo.

Do these props include sound or lights?

Available features vary by build. Common options include blinking LEDs, switches, knobs, battery-powered lighting, and other prop-style electronics.

How do checkout and special requests work?

Available items can be purchased through the Ecwid shop. For special requests or anything not listed, email Ghostworks Lab with the build details so options, price, and timing can be reviewed.

How much is shipping?

A flat shipping rate of $18 is included for all orders unless the product listing or checkout states otherwise. If multiple items are ordered at once, shipping may be combined when possible.

What should I know before buying a DIY kit?

DIY kits require buyer assembly, soldering, wiring, testing, troubleshooting, installation, and fitment. Tools and soldering supplies are not included, and buyer assembly or modification is done at the buyer's own risk.

Does the Lifegard II electronics kit include a shell?

No. Both the DIY and fully assembled Lifegard II versions are electronics only. No shell, case, enclosure, outer body, printed housing, mounting hardware, or cosmetic exterior pieces are included. Buyers must provide and adapt their own shell.

Are these toys or safety equipment?

No. These are fan-made prop electronics for display, cosplay, and collecting. They are not toys, tools, safety devices, or equipment for real paranormal investigation.

Where can I buy available items?

Available items are listed in the Ecwid shop on this page and may also appear on Etsy or eBay. The individual product listing is the final source of truth for price, stock, options, and checkout details.
